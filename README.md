# DSA210-PROJECT
Project Title: 
How does screen time relate to productivity and functioning? 

Proposal: 

Screen-based digital devices have become an essential part of everyday life, especially among university students and young adults. Phones, laptop computers, tablets, and digital platforms are used for communication, entertainment, academic work, and access to information. However, increased screen time may also influence how individuals manage their routines, organize their time, and sustain their productivity. For this reason, examining the relationship between screen time and everyday functioning has become an increasingly significant topic. 

This project aims to explore how screen time is connected to productivity and functioning. More specifically, the study will explore whether differences in screen time are related to measurable behavioral indicators, such as study duration, task completion, procrastination, sleep duration, concentration, distraction, and overall productivity. The purpose of the project is not to make broad claims about all individuals, but rather to examine whether meaningful patterns can be observed through structured behavioral data. 

The data will be collected over a period of approximately 3 to 6 weeks using structured behavioral tracking. Screen time information will be obtained through built-in mobile phone tracking tools. In addition, behavioral variables, such as sleep duration, study time, whether planned tasks were completed, procrastination behavior, and overall productivity level, will also be recorded. These variables are expected to provide a clearer picture of how digital habits may be related to functioning and productivity. 

After the data collection process, the dataset will be examined to identify patterns, trends, and possible relationships between screen time and behavioral indicators. Overall, this project aims to provide a data-driven understanding of how screen time may be associated with productivity and functioning through a structured and measurable dataset.
